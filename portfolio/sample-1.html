A boutique Toronto restaurant applied GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) tactics to its website content, earning citations in ChatGPT and Perplexity for 47 key search queries within 30 days — resulting in a 28% increase in online reservations and 340% boost in AI search visibility. The Problem: Invisible to AI, Losing Customers Daily

Note: This is a fabricated case study for portfolio demonstration purposes. All strategies described are based on real, documented GEO research.

When "Bella Cucina" (a fictional mid-scale Italian restaurant in Toronto's Junction neighbourhood) came to us, they had a beautiful website and strong Google reviews but were completely invisible in AI search results.

When someone asked ChatGPT "best Italian restaurant near High Park" or "date night restaurants Toronto Junction", Bella Cucina was nowhere to be found — despite ranking on page 1 of Google for several of these terms.

Why Traditional SEO Wasn't Enough

The research is clear: fewer than 10% of sources cited by AI engines rank in Google's top 10 for the same queries (eMarketer, 2026). This means your SEO success tells you almost nothing about your AI visibility.

We ran a 10-query AI visibility audit and found:

  • 0 of 10 target queries mentioned Bella Cucina in AI-generated answers
  • Competitors with 40% fewer Google reviews were getting cited by ChatGPT
  • The restaurant's website had zero structured data markup
  • Menu content was written as marketing fluff ("exquisite," "unforgettable") — impossible for AI to extract as facts

What We Did: 5 GEO Tactics in 30 Days

1. Restructured All Content in Answer-First Format

Instead of flowery homepage copy, we rewrote the "About" page to lead with facts AI could extract:

Before: "Experience the unforgettable flavors of authentic Italian cuisine in the heart of Toronto's charming Junction neighbourhood..."

After: "Bella Cucina is a family-owned Italian restaurant in Toronto's Junction neighbourhood, open since 2018. We serve 40+ house-made pasta varieties, source ingredients from 12 Ontario farms, and have a 4.7-star rating across 890 Google reviews."

Princeton University research shows AI engines cite content with 40% higher accuracy when it uses specific, verifiable data points rather than descriptive language.

2. Added Schema Markup to Every Page

We implemented structured data across the site:

  • Restaurant schema (menu, price range, hours, reservations)
  • FAQPage schema on the menu page (answers to "Do they have vegan options?", "Is there parking?")
  • Article schema on blog entries

75% of AI citations come from pages with schema markup (Brandi AI, 2026) — making this the single highest-ROI technical change.

3. Published 4 Topic-Cluster Blog Posts

We created an interlinked content cluster around "Italian dining in Toronto":

  • Post 1: "Best Italian Restaurants in Toronto's West End (2026 Guide)"
  • Post 2: "How to Tell If Pasta Is Actually Made Fresh"
  • Post 3: "Ontario Farm-to-Table: Where Your Ingredients Come From"
  • Post 4: "Date Night Guide: 5 Underrated Toronto Neighbourhoods"

Each post cited 5-8 authoritative sources, included original statistics, and linked to one another — signaling topical authority to AI engines.

4. Built Third-Party Mentions (The Critical Piece)

AI engines trust what others say about a business 19x more than what the business says about itself.

We secured mentions on:

  • Reddit threads discussing Toronto restaurants (r/Toronto, r/AskToronto)
  • Blog guest post on a local food publication
  • Quora answers referencing the restaurant in "Where should I eat..." threads

5. Created a GEO-Optimized Menu Page

Instead of a PDF menu (invisible to AI), we built an HTML menu with:

  • Descriptive dish names with ingredients
  • Origin of key ingredients ("house-made ricotta from Spruce Acres Dairy, Orangeville")
  • Dietary labels (vegan, gluten-free options)
  • Average price per meal ($18-32 CND)
